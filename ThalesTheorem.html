Thale's Theorem

Thale's Theorem says that any triangle inscribed in a semicircle, in our case, triangle DBC, is a right triangle with its hypotenuse equal to the diamter of the semicircle. Can you prove it?

Proof: Let the yellow angle be y, the green angle be g, the blue angle be b, and the purple angle be p. If we can prove that b+p=90, we are done. Let's try!

We can write b and p in terms of y and g, respectively. AD=AB=AC, so triangle ADB and ADC are both isoceles. So p is a base angle of triangle ADB, which makes it equal to (180-g)/2=90-g/2. Similarily, b is 90-y/2. Then p+b=180-(g+y)/2 Ah ha! g+y is 180, since they rest on a straight line. So g+y/2 is 90, and 180-(g+y)/2 is 180-90=90. Proved!
